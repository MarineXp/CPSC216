Watering Setup Option: Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation delivers water directly to plant roots with minimal evaporation loss. It's a great option for maximizing efficiency in garden beds or row crops.

Watering Setup Option: Soaker Hose

Soaker hoses distribute water evenly along their length, perfect for low-maintenance, consistent garden watering. They pair well with automated smart irrigation systems like this one.

Optimal Node Placement for Bluetooth Connection

Place the node within reasonable Bluetooth range of your indoor system. Avoid dense obstacles like thick walls or large metal structures that can interfere with signal strength.

Sensor Placement: Moisture Sensors

Install the two soil moisture sensors in slightly different zones of the garden plot. This helps track variations in soil moisture and ensures that watering is tailored to multiple areas instead of just one spot.

Sensor Placement: Temperature Sensor

Position the soil temperature sensor a few inches below the surface near the root zone of your crops. This allows you to monitor realistic growing conditions and optimize watering based on both moisture and temperature data.

Lighting Analysis: Using the Photoelectric Sensor

Before finalizing your garden layout, temporarily mount the system in different candidate spots. Use the photoelectric sensor to track sunlight exposure throughout the day and select a garden location with optimal lighting for healthy plant growth.

Power Options: Rechargeable Battery Pack

Use a rechargeable battery pack combined with a small solar panel to ensure the system remains powered without needing frequent maintenance, especially during peak growing seasons.
